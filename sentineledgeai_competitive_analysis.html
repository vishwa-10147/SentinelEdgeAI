SentinelEdgeAI strengths

+Only option combining AI-native detection with fully local, no-cloud operation at under $300 hardware cost
+Detects zero-days and novel attack patterns that signature-based tools (pfSense, Meraki) will miss entirely
+GPU-accelerated inference on Jetson keeps latency under 100ms without degrading network throughput
+Fully open, modifiable, and extensible — no vendor lock-in, no subscription dependency
+Adaptive response (log / alert / block / isolate) is more granular than most SME products offer
+Data stays on-premise — critical for regulated industries (healthcare, finance, legal)

Honest limitations

−Baseline AI model needs ~2–4 weeks of real traffic to train meaningfully — cold-start false positive rate is high
−No managed threat intelligence feed at prototype stage — Darktrace and Palo Alto have massive labeled datasets behind them
−Raspberry Pi 5 network throughput cap (~1–2 Gbps) — unsuitable for high-bandwidth enterprise environments without upgrade
−No encrypted-traffic deep inspection at v1 — TLS 1.3 payloads are opaque; relies on metadata and flow behavior only
−Requires internal technical capability to operate — not plug-and-forget like managed appliances
−Hardware failure = full outage — no HA/failover cluster at prototype stage
